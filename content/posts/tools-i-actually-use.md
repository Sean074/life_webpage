There's a genre of blog post that lists tools the author uses, and it's usually aspirational — the tools they wish they used more, or the ones that photograph well. This is a list of what I actually open every day.

## Terminal

Ghostty. It's fast, it renders correctly, and it gets out of the way. Before that I used iTerm2 for years. The switch took a weekend of mild adjustment and now I don't think about it, which is what you want from a terminal.

Zsh with a minimal prompt. I've tried the big frameworks (Oh My Zsh, Starship) and they're too much. A two-line prompt config and some aliases covers ninety percent of it.

## Editor

Neovim. I have a reasonably small config that I've refined over a few years. I don't use it for everything — sometimes VS Code is the right tool, especially when pairing or doing heavy TypeScript work. But for writing and for quick edits, Neovim is faster than anything else I've tried.

## Notes

Plain text files in a directory called `notes/`. No app. No sync service. I use `grep` to search them. The longevity of plaintext is the point — the format will be readable in thirty years.

## What I Don't Use

Task managers, habit trackers, focus timers, second-brain apps. I've tried most of them. The overhead of maintaining the system usually exceeds the value it produces. A small paper notebook and a daily list is more than enough.
